# Mashal — Project Context

## What Mashal Is

**Mashal** (مشعل — Urdu/Persian for "torch" or "beacon," traditionally a symbol of guidance and enlightenment) is a newly founded NGO/initiative bringing practical AI literacy to school students in Pakistan.

## The Problem Being Solved

Target schools — e.g. Dar-e-Arqam, LGS, Learning Alliance/Punjab-tier schools — already have computer labs, devices, and internet access. What they don't have is anyone teaching students to actually *use* AI beyond a chatbot. Students in these schools know how to prompt ChatGPT for homework help, but have no exposure to building websites, automations, CRMs, or using AI practically for career development.

**This is a training/curriculum gap, not an access gap.** The schools are resource-rich; the teaching is missing.

### Framing rule
Do not describe target schools or students as "underprivileged" or frame this as a poverty/access narrative. Use language like "under-taught," "untapped," "overlooked gap," "labs sitting underused." The pitch is about a missing curriculum, not missing resources.

## Current Status (as of this writing)

- **Pre-launch.** No workshops have been run yet.
- No schools have been formally onboarded — one school head has received a one-pager pitch, no confirmation yet.
- No team photos, no testimonials, no partner logos, no real stats exist yet.
- The website needs to build credibility and momentum *without* leaning on results that don't exist yet — it should read as confident about the plan, not dishonest about traction.

## Audience for the Website

1. **School heads / administrators** — need to be convinced to give Mashal a lab session slot. Want to know: what's required of them, what students gain, how much time it takes.
2. **Potential volunteers** — AI/tech people, teachers, designers, social media help.
3. **Parents/students** — may land on the site after a session or referral; want reassurance this is legitimate and useful.

## What Mashal Actually Does (Program Content)

Framed as planned programs, not past workshops:

- **AI Beyond Chat** — intro session reframing what AI is capable of beyond chatting
- **Build With AI** — hands-on: building simple websites, automations, mini-CRMs
- **AI for Careers** — using AI for portfolios, resumes, exploring career paths

## Copy Rules

- Never fabricate stats, workshop numbers, or testimonials.
- Write from the reader's side — a school head cares about logistics and student outcomes, not internal org details.
- Active voice, plain verbs, no filler. Buttons state exactly what happens ("Bring Mashal to your school," not "Learn more").
- Tone: confident, modern, youth-relevant, but credible enough for school administrators — not overly casual, not corporate-stiff.
