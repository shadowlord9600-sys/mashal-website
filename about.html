About

A torch is only useful if you hand it to someone.

Mashal (مشعل) is Urdu for torch. The name is the whole idea: the light isn't the point — passing it on is.

Our story

It started with an answer nobody found strange.

Ask a class of Pakistani teenagers whether they use AI and every hand goes up. Ask what they use it for, and the room says the same thing: homework. Essays. Summaries.

What's striking isn't the answer — it's that nobody in the room thinks the answer is odd. The most capable tool they'll ever be handed has been quietly filed away as a homework shortcut, and no one has told them otherwise.

Mashal exists to tell them otherwise. We're pre-launch: no sessions have run yet, and we'd rather say so than dress it up. What we have is a curriculum and schools worth bringing it to.

Mission & aim

What we're actually here to do.

Mission

Teach students to build with AI — using the labs their schools already have, at no cost to the school.

Practical sessions that fit a normal period, run by people who use these tools for real work. Not a lecture about the future of technology.

Aim

A generation that treats AI as something they direct, not something they ask.

Students who leave school able to make things with these tools — and who know a chatbox was only ever the front door.
